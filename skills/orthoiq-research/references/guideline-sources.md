# OrthoIQ Clinical Guideline Sources

Reference this file when cross-checking citations against current guidelines (Step 5 of SKILL.md).

## Primary Guidelines

### AAOS (American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons)
- Clinical Practice Guidelines: https://www.aaos.org/quality/quality-programs/clinical-practice-guidelines/
- Update cadence: Rolling; check for revisions annually
- Key CPGs relevant to OrthoIQ: ACL injury, rotator cuff, knee OA, carpal tunnel, hip fracture

### AOSSM (American Orthopaedic Society for Sports Medicine)
- Position Statements: https://www.sportsmed.org/research/position-statements
- Key areas: Return to sport, concussion, overuse injuries, biologics

### APTA (American Physical Therapy Association)
- Clinical Practice Guidelines: https://www.apta.org/patient-care/evidence-based-practice-resources/cpg
- Key CPGs: Knee pain, shoulder pain, low back pain, hip pain, ankle sprain

### Cochrane Musculoskeletal Group
- https://musculoskeletal.cochrane.org/our-reviews
- Highest-quality systematic reviews for common MSK conditions

### NICE (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence)
- MSK guidelines: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/conditions-and-diseases/musculoskeletal-conditions
- Note: UK population context; apply to US patients with awareness of healthcare system differences

## Guideline Conflict Protocol

When a PubMed citation contradicts a current guideline:

1. Confirm the guideline version/year — if guideline is >5 years old and citation is recent, note the potential for guideline lag
2. Check if the citation has been cited in any guideline updates or commentary
3. Flag as Grade X in the response with this note:
   > "This study's findings differ from current [ORGANIZATION] guidelines ([Year]). Clinical judgment required. Consider discussing with supervising clinician."

## Known Guideline Gaps (as of skill authoring)

These areas lack formal CPG coverage — use best available evidence with explicit caveat:

- PRP / biologic injections (AAOS position: insufficient evidence)
- Exosome therapy (no formal guideline)
- Blood flow restriction training protocols
- Wearable-guided rehabilitation parameters
- Return-to-sport after cartilage restoration procedures (individualized only)
