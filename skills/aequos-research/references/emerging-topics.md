# AequOs Emerging & High-Volatility Research Topics

These topics have rapidly evolving evidence bases. Apply extra scrutiny when surfacing citations.

## Biologics

### PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma)
- Evidence is highly protocol-dependent — leukocyte-rich vs. leukocyte-poor produce different outcomes
- Knee OA: Most studied application; moderate evidence for short-term pain relief
- Tendinopathy: Growing evidence, especially Achilles and lateral elbow
- Surgical augmentation: Early-stage evidence only
- **Flag**: Industry funding is common in PRP literature — check COI disclosures

### Bone Marrow Aspirate Concentrate (BMAC)
- Limited high-quality human RCTs; most evidence is Level 3-4
- Do not conflate with "stem cell therapy" in lay terms — clarify cell composition

### Exosomes
- Largely preclinical as of 2025-2026; flag any human studies as very early stage
- FDA has not approved exosome products for orthopedic use — always note this

## Surgical Techniques (<5 years follow-up data)

- **BEAR (Bridge-Enhanced ACL Repair)**: Growing RCT data; not yet standard of care
- **Suture tape augmentation** (InternalBrace): Ankle ligament repair; 3-5 year data emerging
- **Robotic-assisted arthroplasty**: Outcomes data maturing; learning curve effects present
- **Cartilage restoration** (MACI, DeNovo, osteochondral allografts): Long-term data limited for newer protocols

---

## Return-to-Sport Science

- **Time-based RTS criteria**: Flag as insufficient alone per current consensus
- **Limb Symmetry Index (LSI)**: 90% threshold is debated — some literature suggests 95%+ for high-demand athletes
- **Psychological readiness**: ACL-RSI and similar tools increasingly cited; note if a study omitted psych readiness assessment

---

## Wearable Technology Integration
*(Relevant for future AequOs Wearable Agent)*

- **Gait analysis wearables**: Growing validation literature; Xsens, APDM Opal, Inertial Labs
- **Asymmetry indices**: Step asymmetry >15% post-ACLR associated with elevated re-injury risk (emerging)
- **Force plate alternatives**: Validated wearable force estimation has limited literature
- **Continuous monitoring**: Fatigue and overtraining detection algorithms are largely proprietary with limited peer review
